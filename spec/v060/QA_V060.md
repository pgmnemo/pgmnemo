## 6. Simulation Validity Caveat

⚠️ **All bench results are from simulation mode.** Key limitations:

1. **TF-IDF ≠ bge-m3**: TF-IDF cosine similarity has less discriminative power than
   bge-m3 semantic embeddings. Score distributions are more uniform → RRF and fusion tend
   to produce more similar rankings → the observed delta may not transfer to real-DB numbers.

2. **RRF benefit condition**: RRF outperforms linear fusion when the two retrieval signals
   (dense vs sparse) have **different score scales and distributions**. bge-m3 produces more
   spread-out cosine scores than TF-IDF, which may make RRF more (or less) beneficial.

3. **Expected vs observed**: PLAN §7 projected +1.5–2pp recall@10. Simulation shows −2.40pp.
   The opposite direction suggests either (a) the simulation proxy is misleading for RRF
   specifically, or (b) Fix-A genuinely regresses on this corpus.

**Recommendation**: Live-DB bench is required to resolve this ambiguity. The NO-GO is based
on available evidence; it does NOT prove Fix-A regresses in production — it proves Fix-A
did not pass the gate under simulation conditions.
